EYA · Quartier polygon labEXPERIMENTAL

Side-by-side comparison of three draft-polygon strategies for the 310 quartiers of Cotonou + Abomey-Calavi. None of these are authoritative. Read README.md for what each version means and known limitations.

Use the drawing controls at the top-left of the map to trace what a quartier should look like.
① Type the quartier name BELOW first, ② click the polygon tool, ③ trace, ④ double-click to finish.
Repeat for each quartier. Use Download to export all drawn polygons as one GeoJSON file.

    Polygon versions

    50 m radius disk around each IGN chef-lieu (311 centroids). No Dijkstra, no water mask, no POI seeding — pure geometric circles. Use this as the fresh starting point.
    Centroid-only nearest-seed partition. Ignores all topology.
    Same as v1 but clipped to IGN arrondissement boundaries. Previous default.
    Multi-source Dijkstra on 100 m grid. Water = forbidden · roads = cheap · POIs named after a quartier act as additional seeds.
    v3 + per-quartier "home land component(s)". Stepping into a component NOT in the quartier's home set costs +8. Bridges (incl. Pont MLK) reconnect land masses. POI evidence in another component adds it to the home set.
    v3 + per-quartier "home side" of each nearby canal/river/stream (>200 m). Stepping from home side into opposite side within 500 m of the polyline costs +25. POI evidence on the other bank flips that waterway to "both" (no penalty). Generic — not hardcoded.
    v5 with a LOCAL fix to rasterize_water() applied only inside the bbox lat 6.370–6.400, lng 2.380–2.415. Inside the bbox: drain/ditch cells that share a 100 m raster cell with a road become claimable (the road wins). Outside the bbox: identical to v5. 33 cells freed; MIDÉDJI +11, TONATO +22, no losses.
    Same bbox as v5c, plus a 2nd fix: when an IGN chef-lieu lands on a water cell (e.g. Yénawa-Fifadji whose chef-lieu sits on the canal), snap it to the nearest non-water cell within 200 m so the quartier gets a real seed. Result: YÉNAWA-FIFADJI 0→16 cells, MIDÉDJI 18→29, TONATO 36→58.
    The 4 polygons you traced on the live lab (POLYGON-1, MIDEDJI, MISSOGBE, GBENONKPO). Loaded from data/eya_user_drawn_quartiers.geojson. Toggle on to compare any of the algorithmic outputs against your interpretation.
    Cells where 2nd-best owner's cost is close to best — fuzzy transition zones.

    Diagnostics

    Road graph: KDTree 15 m node-merge (water-aware — refuses to merge across canals/rivers/lagoons) + roundabout super-nodes + dual-carriageway virtual connectors (40 m, water-aware). From each chef-lieu, Dijkstra walks along OSM roads up to 200 m. Stops at major water (canal · river · stream · lagoon · basin — drains and ditches do not stop) or when a road from another chef-lieu has claimed the node (collision). Each chef-lieu gets a stable hash-based colour (blue hues excluded).
